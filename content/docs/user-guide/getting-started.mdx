## Getting Access to Pulse

There are three ways to start using Pulse:

### Option 1: Ask Your Admin (Recommended for Teams)

If your organization already has Pulse running:

1. Ask your team administrator for an invitation.
2. You will receive an email with a link to join the workspace.
3. Click the link, create your password, and you are in.

### Option 2: Self-Hosted with Docker (For Technical Users)

If you want to run Pulse on your own server:

1. Make sure you have Docker and Docker Compose installed on your machine.
2. Clone the Pulse repository.
3. Run the Docker Compose setup -- this starts all the services Pulse
   needs (the application, the database, and supporting tools).
4. Open your browser and navigate to the local address (typically
   `http://localhost`).
5. Follow the on-screen setup wizard to create your admin account.

> If you need help with Docker setup, ask an engineer on your team or
> refer to the [Contributing Guide](/docs/contributing/getting-started).

### Option 3: Cloud Hosting

Some cloud providers offer managed Pulse hosting. Check with your IT team
to see if a hosted option is available for your organization.

## Account Setup

### Creating Your Account

When you first open Pulse (whether from an invitation link or a fresh
install), you will see a setup page:

1. **Enter your email address** -- this will be your login ID.
2. **Choose a password** -- pick something strong (at least 8 characters,
   mix of letters and numbers).
3. **Enter your name** -- this is how you will appear to other team
   members.
4. **Click "Create Account"** or "Sign Up."

### Signing In

After creating your account:

1. Go to your Pulse URL.
2. Enter your email and password.
3. Click "Sign In."

You will be taken to the dashboard.

### Profile Settings

To update your profile:

1. Click your avatar or initials in the bottom-left corner of the
   dashboard.
2. Select "Account Settings."
3. Here you can:
   - Change your display name
   - Update your password
   - Set your preferred language
   - Configure notification preferences

## Workspace Basics

### What Is a Workspace?

A workspace is your team's shared environment in Pulse. Everything you
build -- apps, knowledge bases, model configurations -- lives inside
your workspace.

Think of a workspace like a shared folder: everyone on the team can see
and work with the contents, but people outside the team cannot.

### Switching Workspaces

If you belong to multiple workspaces:

1. Click the workspace name in the top-left corner of the dashboard.
2. A dropdown will show all workspaces you belong to.
3. Click the one you want to switch to.

### Inviting Team Members

If you are an Owner or Admin:

1. Go to "Settings" (gear icon in the sidebar).
2. Click "Members."
3. Click "Invite Member."
4. Enter their email address and choose their role:
   - **Admin** -- can manage settings and members
   - **Editor** -- can create and edit apps
   - **Normal** -- can view and use apps
   - **Dataset Operator** -- can manage knowledge bases only
5. Click "Send Invitation."

They will receive an email with a link to join.

## Dashboard Navigation

When you sign in, you land on the dashboard. Here is what you will see:

### Sidebar (Left Side)

The sidebar is your main navigation:

| Section       | What It Contains                             |
| ------------- | -------------------------------------------- |
| **Apps**      | All your apps (chatbots, workflows, etc.)    |
| **Knowledge** | Your knowledge bases (document collections)  |
| **Plugins**   | Installed plugins and the plugin marketplace |
| **Tools**     | Custom tools available to your workflows     |

### Top Bar

The top bar shows:

- **Workspace name** -- click to switch workspaces
- **Search** -- find apps, knowledge bases, or settings quickly
- **Notifications** -- alerts about shared resources or system events

### Main Area

The main area changes based on what you select in the sidebar:

- **Apps view**: Shows a grid or list of all your apps. Each app card
  shows its name, type, and last modified date.
- **Knowledge view**: Shows your knowledge bases with document counts
  and status.
- **Settings**: Workspace configuration, member management, model
  providers.

### Creating Your First App

From the Apps view:

1. Click the "Create App" button (usually in the top-right corner).
2. Choose an app type:
   - **Chatbot** -- for conversational apps
   - **Workflow** -- for automation pipelines
   - **Agent** -- for autonomous AI assistants
3. Give your app a name and optional description.
4. Click "Create."

You will be taken to the app editor where you can configure your app.

## Configuring Your First Model Provider

Before you can build anything useful, you need to connect Pulse to an
AI model provider. This is like giving Pulse the keys to an AI service.

### What Is a Model Provider?

A model provider is a company that runs AI models and lets you use them
through an API (a way for software to talk to other software). Common
providers include:

| Provider  | Popular Models            | Best Known For                    |
| --------- | ------------------------- | --------------------------------- |
| OpenAI    | GPT-4, GPT-4o             | General-purpose AI, widely used   |
| Anthropic | Claude 3.5, Claude 3 Opus | Long documents, careful reasoning |
| Google    | Gemini Pro, Gemini Ultra  | Multimodal (text + images)        |
| Mistral   | Mistral Large, Mixtral    | Open-source models, fast          |
| Meta      | Llama 3                   | Open-source, self-hostable        |

### Getting an API Key

Before connecting a provider, you need an API key from them:

1. Go to the provider's website (e.g., platform.openai.com).
2. Create an account if you do not have one.
3. Navigate to "API Keys" or "Credentials."
4. Generate a new API key.
5. Copy the key -- you will need it in the next step.

> **Important**: Treat your API key like a password. Do not share it
> publicly or paste it into documents. Pulse encrypts your key when you
> save it.

### Adding a Provider to Pulse

1. Go to "Settings" in the sidebar.
2. Click "Model Providers."
3. You will see a list of supported providers. Find the one you want and
   click "Set Up."
4. Paste your API key into the field.
5. Click "Save."

Pulse will verify your key by making a small test request. If everything
works, you will see a green checkmark.

### Verifying It Works

After adding a provider:

1. Go back to "Settings" > "Model Providers."
2. Click on the provider you just added.
3. You should see a list of available models with green status indicators.

If any model shows a red status, your API key may not have access to
that specific model. Check your provider's billing and access settings.
