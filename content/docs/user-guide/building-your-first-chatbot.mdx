## What We Are Building

We are going to build a friendly assistant chatbot that helps answer
questions about a fictional product called "Acme Widgets." This is a
simple Chat mode app -- the user types messages and the AI responds in
a conversational style.

The same steps apply whether you are building a customer support bot, a
personal assistant, or any other conversational application.

---

## Step 1: Create the App

1. From the dashboard, click **"Create App"** in the top-right corner.
2. Select **"Chatbot"** as the app type.
3. A dialog will appear asking you to choose between:
   - **Basic** -- a simple chatbot with one AI model
   - **Workflow-based** -- a chatbot powered by a visual workflow
4. Select **"Basic"** for now (we will cover workflows in the next
   chapter).
5. Give your app a name, for example: "Acme Support Bot."
6. Optionally add a description: "Helps customers learn about Acme
   Widgets."
7. Click **"Create."**

You will be taken to the app editor. This is where you configure how
your chatbot behaves.

---

## Step 2: Choose a Model

The model is the AI brain behind your chatbot. You need to select which
model to use.

1. In the app editor, find the **"Model"** section (usually at the top
   or in a settings panel on the right).
2. Click the model dropdown. You will see a list of available models
   from the providers you configured in the previous chapter.
3. Choose a model. If you are not sure which to pick:
   - **For general use**: GPT-4o or Claude 3.5 Sonnet are good all-around
     choices.
   - **For lower cost**: GPT-4o-mini or Claude 3.5 Haiku are cheaper but
     still capable.
4. Leave the other model settings (temperature, max tokens) at their
   defaults for now.

> **Tip**: You can change the model at any time. Try different models
> later to see which one gives the best answers for your use case.

---

## Step 3: Write a System Prompt

The **system prompt** (also called "instructions" or "persona") tells
the AI how to behave. This is the most important part of building a
good chatbot.

1. Find the **"System Prompt"** or **"Instructions"** text area in the
   editor.
2. Write instructions that define:
   - **Who the AI is**: "You are a helpful customer support agent for
     Acme Widgets."
   - **How it should behave**: "Be friendly, concise, and professional."
   - **What it should know**: "Acme Widgets come in three sizes: Small,
     Medium, and Large. They are made of recycled aluminum."
   - **What it should not do**: "Do not make up information. If you do
     not know the answer, say so."

Here is an example system prompt:

```
You are a friendly customer support agent for Acme Widgets.

About Acme Widgets:
- We sell widgets in three sizes: Small ($10), Medium ($25), and Large ($50)
- All widgets are made from recycled aluminum
- Free shipping on orders over $50
- 30-day money-back guarantee
- Support hours: Monday-Friday, 9 AM to 5 PM EST

Your guidelines:
- Be warm, helpful, and concise
- Answer product questions accurately
- For order issues, ask for the order number
- If you do not know the answer, say: "Let me connect you with a specialist"
- Never make up product information
```

3. Paste or type your system prompt into the text area.

> **Tip**: The more specific your instructions, the better the AI
> performs. Vague instructions lead to inconsistent behavior. Include
> specific facts, rules, and examples.

## Step 4: Test in the Debug Panel

Before sharing your chatbot with anyone, test it yourself using the
built-in debug panel.

1. Look for a **"Debug"** or **"Preview"** button (usually on the
   right side of the editor, or at the top).
2. Click it to open the debug panel. This shows a chat interface where
   you can talk to your bot.
3. Type a test message, for example: "Hi, how much does a large widget
   cost?"
4. Press Enter and wait for the response.
5. Check the response:
   - Is it accurate? (The large widget should be $50.)
   - Is the tone right? (Should be friendly and professional.)
   - Does it follow your instructions?

### Test Different Scenarios

Try several types of messages to make sure your bot handles them well:

| Test Message                   | What You Are Checking                   |
| ------------------------------ | --------------------------------------- |
| "Hello!"                       | Greeting response                       |
| "How much is a medium widget?" | Product knowledge                       |
| "Do you ship internationally?" | Handling unknown information            |
| "I want to return my order"    | Following instructions for order issues |
| "Tell me a joke"               | Staying on topic                        |
| "asdfgh"                       | Handling gibberish gracefully           |

## Step 5: Adjust and Improve

Based on your testing, you will likely want to make adjustments:

### If the AI Is Too Verbose

Add to your system prompt: "Keep your responses under 3 sentences unless
the user asks for more detail."

### If the AI Makes Things Up

Add: "Only answer questions based on the information I have provided.
If a question is outside your knowledge, say so."

### If the Tone Is Wrong

Be more specific about tone: "Write as if you are talking to a friend --
casual but professional. Use short sentences. Avoid jargon."

### If It Does Not Know Enough

Add more facts to the system prompt. The more specific information you
include, the better the AI can answer questions about your product.

> **For large amounts of information**: Instead of cramming everything
> into the system prompt, use a Knowledge Base. See
> [Chapter 06: Knowledge Bases](/docs/user-guide/knowledge-bases) to learn how.

### Adjusting Model Settings

If the AI's responses feel too creative or too robotic, try adjusting
the **temperature** setting:

- **Lower temperature** (closer to 0): More predictable, factual,
  consistent responses. Good for customer support.
- **Higher temperature** (closer to 1): More creative, varied responses.
  Good for brainstorming or creative writing.

## Step 6: Publish Your Chatbot

Once you are happy with how your chatbot behaves, it is time to share it.

### Share Within Your Team

1. Click **"Publish"** or **"Save & Publish"** in the top-right corner.
2. Your chatbot is now live. Team members with access to your workspace
   can find it in the Apps list.

### Share via Link

1. After publishing, look for a **"Share"** or **"Access"** section.
2. You can generate a **public link** that anyone can use to chat with
   your bot, even if they do not have a Pulse account.
3. Copy the link and share it via email, Slack, or wherever your users
   are.

### Embed in a Website

1. In the Share section, look for **"Embed"** options.
2. You will find a small snippet that you can paste into your website's
   HTML.
3. This creates a chat widget that appears on your site, letting
   visitors talk to your bot directly.

### Publish as an API

1. In the Share section, look for **"API Access."**
2. You will find an API key and documentation for how other software
   can talk to your chatbot.
3. This is useful if you want to connect your chatbot to other tools
   like Slack, Teams, or a custom application.
